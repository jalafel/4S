1. Code as Race

It's difficult to speak about queer theory within referring to critical race theory. Within STS it's easy to get by without mentioning race outside of facial recognition problems. Nevertheless we must consider how the basis of most technologies is constructed and outsourced at the low-paid or unpaid labour of people of colour.
  • Between 1965 and 1975, 922 Navajo women were employed to work on circuit assembly in Shiprock, New Mexico
  • "Indian" women "have" an affinity toward textiles and craft
  • Narrative obscures competitive capitalistic heart
Story is constructed to justify this labour: an inherent talent, propsensity due to craft/beadwork and deft, nimble fingers of women of colour. This alludes to some art or talent to craft, thought the work remains to be Marxist and procedural in construction. The uplifting narrative obscures the low pay, working conditions, and inital reasonings for hiring on Navajo land: low taxation. At its heart, a capitalistic venture. Another way obscuring happens is through modularity.

UNIX principles

  • "Modularity"; abstracts and obscures away complexity
  • "Separation of Concerns"; each module is used for one thing only
  • Reflects the identity politics which fractured solidarity between unionizing labour workers and black and POC activists
At the same time, during those years Bell Labs was conceiving of a set of rules that would be dogmatically followed by most programmers decades later, and to the present day. Those are the UNIX principles. UNIX is an open source program that has now become the basis for most operating systems today. (Including tablets, smartphones, and CPUs). There are many principles, but today we will focus on just one: modularity and separation of concerns. Through these two principles which abstracts away much of the complexity of code. The development of this logic also corresponds to a time in which idenity politics began to overtake class consciousness: labour union workers and civil rights activists were working together, but slowly became divisive. It is impossible to say that the logic of UNIX has become reflected in our actions and thoughts -- or merely it's a manifestation of its ethos. Nevertheless, let us consider the tools we use and the logic that is embedded within their construction and ultimately materiality.

2. Code as Masculinity Culture Contests (MCCs)

  • MCCs observed in open-source communities
  • How "open" is open-source if only 1.5% of participants are women? (Nafus, 2011)
  • Belief in a moral liberal ideology / libertarian fantasy; minimizes gender differences
  • Firefox =/= 100% "open"
- MCCs are a way to sociologically describe the culture which prioritizes hegemonic masculinity and masculine traits to be expressed in order to succeed in a social group. - Rape jokes, aggressive comments and lack of support to new-comers to code. - Often women are deferred to do "softer" work such as writing documentation, rather than writing code. Mentorship for women were often seen as a dating opportunity or free emotional labour. - Open-source co-opted by large companies; who behave as stakeholders in considerations of open-source decisions. - Black-box of Netflix that Firefox had to adopt to compete.

Cybernetics and Opacity

Haraway's Cybernetics

  • Communication = control
  • "obscuring the relations of domination in production"
  • "obscures the competitive bedrock of captialism w phenomena like altruism and liberal corporate responsbility in transnational enterprises"
We often speak of intersectionality in the context of those who are oppressed. But Haraway urges us also to consider how intersectionality exists also in those who are oppressors. For Haraway, the informatics of domination is a part of the technology within cybernetics which controls and obscures. By revealing these aspects of code through the lens of queerness we are offered a way to realize our position within relation with the technology we use everyday.

Glissant's Right to Opacity

  • Transparency =/= opacity
  • "Understanding" is premised on transparency which requires scales and measurements andlead to reduction
  • "Human behaviours are fractal in nature"
Opacity is coming to see and hold everything for what it is, without reduction. how can we begin to understand when we are using scales and measures in order to extract and categorize each horror? each triumph? each human body? poignant how glissant begins on the stories of the boat slave Glissant brings this up in the context of the trans-atlantic slave trade, when each human body was measured by their strength, child for their potential and sold for a calculated cost. For Glissant, the philosophy of opacity, is not to understand, but to bare witness to the relations; and what those relationships reveal.

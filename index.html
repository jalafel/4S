Nevertheless we must consider how the basis of most technologies is constructed and outsourced at the low-paid or unpaid labour of people of colour. Present & Past.
  • Between 1965 and 1975, 922 Navajo women were employed to work on circuit assembly in Shiprock, New Mexico
  • Indian women "have" an affinity toward textiles and craft
  • Racialization is a process and not a product; obscures
Story is constructed to justify this labour: an inherent talent, propsensity due to craft/beadwork and deft, nimble fingers of women of colour. This alludes to some art or talent to craft, thought the work remains to be Marxist and procedural in construction. UNIX principles

  • "Modularity" - abstracts and obscures away complexity
  • "Seperation of Concerns" - each module is used for one thing only
  • Reflects the identity politics which fractured solidarity between unionizing labour workers and black and PoC activists
At the same time, during those years Bell Labs was conceiving of a set of rules that would be dogmatically followed by most programmers decades later, and to the present day. Those are the UNIX principles. UNIX is an open source program that has now become the basis for most operating systems today. (Including tablets, smartphones, and cpus). There are 12 principles, but today we will focus on just one: Modularity and single-purpose UNIX principle of : modularity and single use has abstracted away much code the development of this logic also corresponds to a time in which idenity politics began to overtake class consciousness: labour union workers and civil rights activists were working together, but slowly became divisive. disciplines in the academy also became separated. it is impossible to say that the logic of UNIX has become reflected in our actions and thoughts due to its wide distribution -- or merely it's a manifestation, like many other inventions, music, art, sciences, of the ethos of the time. nevertheless, i want to point one to consider the tools we make and use and how they are derived. the logic that is embedded within them. whether or not its materiality and construction affect us. Cybernetics and Opacity

Haraway's Cybernetics

  • Communication = control
  • "obscuring the relations of domination in production"
  • "obscures the competitive bedrock of captialism w phenomena like altruism and liberal corporate responsbility in transnational enterprises"
We often speak of intersectionality in the context of those who are oppressed. But Haraway urges us also to consider how intersectionality exists also in those who are oppressors. - From Haraway, the system of domination appears at once both infinite and immovable, and fragile, brittle, and could collapse at any second. - This paradox is the nature of domination for it's not static, in fact it could - By revealing these aspects of code through the lens of queerness we are offered a way to challenge te existing structures, or at least how we understand more fully how we exist within relation with the technology we use everyday - Baudrillard writes, theorists must imagine another political economic based on more than just the human capacity to produce: they must “find a realm beyond economic value.” - Glissant writes that opacity is about responsibility for healing the entire rhizome (Deleuzian) - example: Darwin's competitive biological paradigm has been adopted by our algorithms and computer systems - example from all previous slides (remind audience)

Glissant's Right to Opacity

  • Transparency =/= opacity
  • "Understanding" is premised on transparency which requires scales and measurements andlead to reduction
  • “Human behaviours are fractal in nature”
Opacity is coming to see and hold everything for what it is, without reduction. how can we begin to understand when we are using scales and measures in order to extract and categorize each horror? each triumph? each human body? poignant how glissant begins on the stories of the boat slave Glissant brings this up in the context of the trans-atlantic slave trade and his origin and connection to the caribbean, ... when each human body was measured by their strength, child for their potential and sold for a calculated cost. For Glissant, the philosophy of opacity, is not to understand, but to bare witness to the relations; and what those relationships reveal.
